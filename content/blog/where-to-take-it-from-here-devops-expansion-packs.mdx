Now that you have built the "Standard Model" of a DevOps pipeline, the plumbing is working.

From here, you can take this lab in several professional directions depending on which part of the job excites you most.

If you want the full foundation first, start with [Mac to Cloud: Building an Automated Delivery Pipeline](/blog/mac-to-cloud-pipeline).

Here are four expansion packs for your Lima lab, ranked from easiest to most advanced.

<h2 id="monitoring-observability">Expansion 1: Monitoring & Observability (The "Dashboard" Step)</h2>

In production, you do not wait for a user to tell you the site is down; you watch the metrics.

**The Goal:** Add extra services to your `compose.yaml` for Prometheus (to collect data) and Grafana (to visualize dashboards).

**What you'll learn:** How to monitor CPU, RAM, and uptime-related metrics for your Nginx and Postgres containers.

**Challenge:** Can you build a dashboard that shows how many hits your Nginx server has received?

<h2 id="reverse-proxy-ssl">Expansion 2: Reverse Proxy & SSL (The "Security" Step)</h2>

Right now, your site is "naked" on port 80. Real sites use HTTPS and a reverse proxy.

**The Goal:** Add Traefik or a second Nginx container to act as a front door.

**What you'll learn:** How to handle domain names, SSL certificates, and routing traffic between different apps.

**Challenge:** Can you set it up so `app1.localhost` goes to one container and `app2.localhost` goes to another?

<h2 id="kubernetes-scale-step">Expansion 3: Kubernetes (The "Scale" Step)</h2>

Docker Compose is great for one machine, but Kubernetes (K8s) is how many production platforms run at scale.

**The Goal:** Instead of standard Docker orchestration, install K3s (a lightweight Kubernetes distribution) inside your Lima VM.

**What you'll learn:** The difference between a container and a pod, plus how Kubernetes self-heals by replacing failed workloads.

**Challenge:** Can you kill your Nginx pod and watch Kubernetes bring it back to life in seconds?

<h2 id="terraform-cloud-step">Expansion 4: Infrastructure as Code: Terraform (The "Cloud" Step)</h2>

You used Lima to build a VM on your Mac. Terraform is used to build and manage infrastructure on AWS, Google Cloud, Azure, and more.

**The Goal:** Install the Terraform CLI on your Mac and use it to manage your Lima VM instead of `limactl`.

**What you'll learn:** How to manage infrastructure across different providers with one language (HCL).

**Challenge:** Can you use one command to spin up your VM, install Docker, and deploy your GitHub repo?

<h2 id="closing-thought">Closing Thought</h2>

At this point, you are no longer learning random tools. You are composing a professional platform skill set one layer at a time.

Pick one expansion pack, ship a small milestone this week, and keep the lab iterative.
