DevOps is more than a toolset. It is a way of building and operating software through collaboration, shared ownership, and fast feedback between development, operations, and other stakeholders.

This course introduces the foundations and history of DevOps, the human side of delivery (culture, communication, and cross-functional teamwork), and core technical practices such as CI/CD and automation. It also covers LEAN thinking, continuous improvement, and the key architectures and metrics used to evaluate delivery performance and system quality.

The format is narrative rather than lab-based, using realistic team challenges to show how DevOps principles solve real-world problems.

### What you'll learn

- Understand why large IT organizations struggle to deliver value and how DevOps addresses those root causes
- Learn about the people dimension: culture, shared ownership, and cross-functional team design
- Understand the process dimension: automation maturity, LEAN principles, and continuous improvement
- Explore the product and technology dimension: CI/CD pipelines, IaC, and observability
- Recognize the antipatterns that undermine DevOps adoptions and how to avoid them
- Apply key delivery metrics (DORA metrics and others) to evaluate team and system health
