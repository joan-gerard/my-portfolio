# Role

## Role Summary

Avanade is hiring a **Frontend Developer** to build modern, responsive, and intuitive web applications. The role is frontend-focused but contributes to end-to-end solutions, with ownership over UI quality, frontend architecture, and seamless digital experiences across web platforms.

**Expected start date:** mid-August 2026.

### Key Responsibilities

- Design, develop, and maintain web applications using **React, Angular, Vue.js**, or similar frameworks
- Build **responsive, accessible**, and user-friendly interfaces across devices
- Collaborate with **UX/UI designers** to turn designs into production-ready applications
- Develop **reusable components**, design systems, and scalable frontend architectures
- Integrate frontend apps with **APIs and backend services**
- Optimize **performance, usability, and accessibility**
- Work with modern **state management**, component-based architectures, and frontend tooling
- Participate in **DevOps practices**: CI/CD, automated testing, and cloud deployments
- Collaborate with backend developers and stakeholders to deliver reliable, scalable solutions

### Qualifications

- ~**5 years** of frontend development experience
- Strong **JavaScript/TypeScript** skills
- Hands-on experience with **React, Angular, or Vue.js**
- Experience building **responsive and accessible** web applications
- Knowledge of **frontend testing** frameworks and best practices
- Familiarity with **cloud platforms** (Azure, AWS, or Google Cloud)
- Understanding of **REST APIs**, authentication flows, and web security
- Experience working in **Agile** environments

### Desired Skills

- **Full stack development** experience (listed as a bonus)

### Traits for Success

- Curious and eager to learn new technologies
- Collaborative team player who shares knowledge
- Takes ownership from idea to implementation
- Solution-oriented; breaks down complex problems into practical steps
- Clear communicator with technical and non-technical stakeholders
- Strong **Swedish and English** (spoken and written) for customer and stakeholder communication

# Call strategy

Position yourself as a hands-on Frontend Developer with lead-level ownership, but do not over-emphasize leadership or DevOps.
For this recruiter call, the main risk is Swedish, because the role description explicitly asks for strong communication skills in Swedish and English. Handle that honestly and early if they ask.

## Core Message

Use this as your anchor:

> “I’m looking for a hands-on Frontend Developer role. My strongest area is React, Next.js, Remix, TypeScript, accessibility, performance, and frontend quality. Even though my previous title included Lead Developer / Project Lead, I was still coding daily and I enjoy being close to implementation.”

That matches your CV well because you describe building SSR applications with React, Next.js, Remix, and TypeScript, while also doing PR reviews, clean architecture, testing, accessibility, and delivery work.

## Interview Prep Notes

- Emphasize frontend craft: component architecture, design systems, accessibility, and performance
- Be ready to discuss framework experience (React/Angular/Vue) and how you integrate with APIs
- Highlight Agile collaboration with designers, backend teams, and stakeholders
- Mention CI/CD, testing, and cloud familiarity if relevant
- Full stack experience is a differentiator but the role is frontend-led

## Q&A

### 1. “Tell me briefly about yourself.”

> “I’m Joan, a Software Engineer based in Stockholm.
>
> My strongest area is frontend development with React, Next.js, Remix, and TypeScript.
>
> In my previous role at Netdesignr, I worked as Lead Developer / Project Lead, but I was still hands-on with coding, PR reviews, accessibility, performance, and frontend quality.
>
> I’m now looking for a hands-on Frontend Developer role where I can contribute to modern web applications, collaborate with designers and backend developers, and help deliver clean, scalable user experiences.”

### 2. “Why are you interested in this role?”

> “The role sounds **close to the kind of frontend work I enjoy**: building modern web applications, reusable components, responsive and accessible interfaces, and working across design, backend, and delivery.
>
> I also like that the role mentions frontend architecture, performance, testing, APIs, and cloud-based technologies, because those are areas I’ve worked with and want to keep developing.”

### 3. “Why Avanade?”

> “I’m interested in Avanade because it combines consulting, technology, cloud, and client-facing delivery. I like the idea of working on real business problems while still being hands-on technically. I’m also open to consulting environments because I’ve worked with clients before and I’m comfortable translating technical topics clearly for different stakeholders.”

### 4. “Are you looking for a developer role or a lead role?”

This is important. Say this clearly:

> “For this move, I’m looking for a hands-on developer role. My previous lead experience is useful because I can take ownership, communicate clearly, and support quality, but I’m not only looking for a management or lead position. I want to be close to the code.”

### 5. “What is your Swedish level?”

Be honest. Do not soften it too much.

> “At the moment, I don’t speak Swedish. English is my working language, and I’m comfortable communicating with technical and non-technical stakeholders in English. I saw that Swedish was mentioned in the role description, so I’d be happy to understand whether Swedish is a strict requirement for this specific position or more of a preference depending on the client project.”

This is the best answer. It is honest, professional, and puts the recruiter in a position to clarify.

### 6. “Are you comfortable in a consulting/client-facing role?”

> “Yes. I’m open to both consulting and product environments. In my previous role, I worked closely with clients and internal teams, so I’m comfortable with stakeholder communication, clarifying requirements, and translating technical topics into practical next steps.”

Your CV supports this because you were the liaison between clients and engineering and translated technical concepts into actionable plans.

### 7. “What frontend frameworks have you used?”

> “My strongest experience is with React, Next.js, Remix, and TypeScript. I’ve built responsive, server-side rendered, accessible applications and worked with modern frontend architecture, reusable components, and performance improvements. I’m strongest in React, but I’m open to working with other modern frontend frameworks if needed.”

### 8. “Have you worked with Angular or Vue?”

> “My production experience is mainly React-based rather than Angular or Vue. But the core frontend principles transfer well: component architecture, state management, API integration, accessibility, testing, and performance. If the project uses Angular or Vue, I’d be comfortable ramping up.”

### 9. “Have you worked with testing?”

> “Yes. I’ve worked with frontend quality processes including PR reviews, visual testing, and automated testing workflows. I care about preventing regressions, especially around critical user flows, responsive layouts, accessibility, and reusable components.”

### 10. “Have you worked with cloud or DevOps?”

Since you do not want to emphasize DevOps unless asked, keep it short:

> “Yes, to some extent. I’ve worked with serverless AWS before, and I understand how frontend delivery connects with deployment, CI/CD, and cloud-based environments. But for this role, I’d mainly position myself as a frontend developer with good awareness of cloud and delivery practices.”

### 11. “Can you explain the career break?”

Keep this calm and short.

> “Yes. Since May 2025, I’ve used the time for professional development and portfolio work. I’ve been strengthening my frontend architecture, cloud, and performance knowledge while building projects end-to-end. So it has been a deliberate development period, and I’m now ready to return to a team environment.”

Your CV already frames the break as professional development focused on frontend architecture, cloud-based web platforms, AI-assisted development, and performance optimization.

### 12. “What salary are you looking for?”

> “I’m looking for around 45,000 SEK per month. I’m open to discussing the full package depending on the role, expectations, and benefits.”

### 13. “When can you start?”

The role says expected start is mid-August 2026.

> “Mid-August works well for me.”

### 14. “Are you open to hybrid/on-site work?”

Use this unless you have a stricter preference:

> “Yes, I’m open to hybrid work. I’m based in Stockholm, so I’m comfortable discussing the expected office setup.”

## Questions you should ask the recruiter

For a 10-minute call, ask only two.

### Best question 1 — Swedish requirement

> “I noticed Swedish is mentioned in the role description. Is Swedish a strict requirement for this specific role, or does it depend on the client/project?”

This is the most important question.

### Best question 2 — next steps

> “What would the next steps look like after this initial call?”

### Optional third question

Only if there is time:

> “Do you know whether the first project would be React-focused, or could it involve Angular or Vue depending on the client?”

## What not to say

### Avoid

> “I’m transitioning into DevOps.”

That may confuse the recruiter. Only mention DevOps if they ask about your career break or cloud/CI/CD.

### Avoid

> “I was mostly leading projects.”

### Instead

> “I had lead responsibilities, but I stayed hands-on.”

### Avoid

> “I don’t speak Swedish, but I’m learning.”

Only say that if you are actually learning. Better:

> “At the moment, I don’t speak Swedish. English is my working language.”

## Your 10-minute call structure

### Minute 1–2: Intro

Use your 45-second pitch.

### Minute 2–4: Motivation

Explain why the role fits: React, TypeScript, frontend quality, accessibility, APIs, collaboration.

### Minute 4–6: Recruiter checks basics

Expect salary, availability, Swedish, location, hybrid, right to work.

### Minute 6–8: Clarify concerns

Be ready for career break and why you want a developer role after lead experience.

### Minute 8–10: Your questions

Ask about Swedish requirement and next steps.
